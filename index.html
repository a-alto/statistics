STATISTICS & CYBERSECURITY

What is statistics?

Encyclopedia Britannica: «Statistics is the science of collecting, analyzing, presenting, and interpreting data.»

«Governmental needs for census data as well as information about a variety of economic activities provided much of the early impetus for the field of statistics. Currently the need to turn the large amounts of data available in many applied fields into useful information has stimulated both theoretical and practical developments in statistics.»

Statistics is a branch of mathematics, its main concerns are:

  • Collection of data.
  • Organization and summarization of data into aggregates.
  • Analysis of data to extract trends and other metrics.
  • Interpretation of data, trends, and metrics to give meaning and understand them.

Why can it be useful for cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity heavily relies on probabilistic and stochastic methods in order to effectively model threats and understand what are the main concerns experts have to deal with. Some of the key subfields of cybersecurity in which statistics is employed are, for instance, risk assessment, incident forensics, and threat detection.
